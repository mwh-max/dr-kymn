Who Qualifies?

Kentucky Medicaid & KCHIP — 2025. Plain‑language income limits with links to official sources.

Income‑based eligibility (MAGI)

These groups use FPL% and MAGI rules. Keep scrolling for Aged/Blind/Disabled (ABD) rules.

  • Adults (ages 19–64)

    Up to 138% FPL · MAGI rules.

  • Pregnancy

    Up to 200% FPL. Includes coverage through 12 months postpartum.

  • Children & teens (under 19)

    Medicaid: up to 200% FPL · KCHIP (CHIP): up to 218% FPL.

What is FPL? What does %FPL mean?

Federal Poverty Level (FPL) is a yearly income baseline set by the federal government. Your %FPL depends on household size and your MAGI. 2025 examples: 1 person = $15,650/year; 3 people = $26,650/year.

Aged, Blind, or Disabled (ABD)

ABD Medicaid does not use a flat FPL%. Kentucky applies SSI‑related income and resource rules; some applicants may qualify via spenddown.

  • ABD rules (summary)

    Monthly income: very low SSI‑related standard (see CHFS).
    Resources: about $2,000 (individual).
    Spenddown: may apply; check CHFS.
