What If I'm Denied?

A denial isn’t the end. Here’s what it means, and what you can do next.

Why denials happen

Common reasons include:

  • Income too high under program rules
  • Missing documents or deadlines
  • Not in an eligible category (age, disability, pregnancy, etc.)

Your options

Plain-language next steps you can take:

  • 1) Double-check the letter

    The notice should explain why you were denied. Keep the letter — you’ll need it for appeals or reapplications.

  • 2) Fix small issues

    If the problem was missing paperwork or an error, reapply with the correct information or send in what’s missing.

  • 3) File an appeal

    You have the right to appeal. Follow the instructions in your denial letter. Appeals are time-sensitive — usually you must act within 30 days.

  • 4) Look at other programs

    If you don’t qualify for Medicaid, you may be eligible for Kentucky Children’s Health Insurance (KCHIP) or subsidized coverage on the HealthCare.gov Marketplace.

  • 5) Get free help

    You don’t have to figure it out alone. Kentucky Assisters can walk you through appeals or other options.
